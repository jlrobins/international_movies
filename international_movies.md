> I can recommend some Hungarian stuff, but I think you will be lost, because the socialist era definitely left its mark on our society, so without that knowledge, I don't know how much you will be able to understand, but I'll try to recommend newer movies.
> 
> So, if I have to pick only one that I liked the most, then it's [Lesson Learned](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt30807200/) (2024) - or Fekete Pont in Hungarian - Hungarian 12 year old kid's family moves back to Hungary from Germany and we follow the kid in his new school. This is a critique about our current education system. But because you are Canadian, maybe you would find more interesting the movie called [White Palms](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0464955/) (2006) - or Fehér Tenyér -, where a Hungarian gymnast moves to Canada to teach kids and he uses the same abusive teaching techniques that his coaches used on him.
> 
> If you are interested in the socialist era, then [The Witness](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0065067/) (1969) - or A Tanú - is a must watch. It was banned immediately because it was making fun of the system.
> 
> From the newer movies, I can recommend [Hope You Die Next Time:-)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8529512/) (2018)- or Remélem legközelebb sikerül meghalnod:) - which is about sexting between a student and her English teacher. You can also check out [I Accidentally Wrote a Book](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt27811640/) (2024) - or Véletlenül Írtam egy Könyvet -, it's about grief. A teen daughter lost her mother at a younger age and starts to forget her mother, while her father start moving on. For a bit darker tone, you could watch [Well](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4606770/) (2016) - or Kút - and this is about a gas station near the border where a van arrives with 4 prostitutes. If you are interested in documentary, then we had [Pelikan Blue](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt10133702/) (2023) - or Kék Pelikán. It's an animation documentary based on a true story about forging international train tickets. And lastly from the contemporary batch, [Liza the Fox-Fairy](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2210769/) (2015) - or Liza, a Rókatündér -, which is a dark comedy where the main lead can see a dead Japanese pop star who starts killing the main lead's partners.
> 
> Here's some "older" titles that are either popular or award winning. The first one is [Son of Saul](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3808342/) (2015) - or Saul Fia -, it follows a Jew in a concentration camp during the WWII. It won an Oscar, that's why I mention it. The next one is [Control](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0373981/) (2003) - or Kontroll -, it's hard to describe it, because it's genre mix, but we follow a ticket inspector during his work. And the last one is [A Kind of America](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0303184/) (2002) - or Valami Amerika - and it's about a Hungarian film director who wants to direct his first movie, but he doesn't have the money for it. Surprisingly he receives an e-mail from an American film producer and he would like to meet. This is a comedy.

> My personal favorite Russian film of all time, though, is *An Unfinished Piece for Mechanical Piano* (1977) by Nikita Mikhalkov.
